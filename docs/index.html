What a FIPS certificate actually tells you

A CMVP certificate shows that a defined cryptographic module version, in a specific configuration, operational environment, and approved mode, completed validation. It does not by itself show that the product running today still uses that validated state. This corpus reads the public record for what it establishes, where correspondence to a deployment becomes uncertain, and what evidence a reviewer should request next.

The question this answers

A certificate is necessary evidence of deployed FIPS compliance, but not sufficient evidence that the deployed cryptographic function still matches the validated state. Establishing that runs a chain, and any unresolved step turns into an evidence request.

certificate found→deployed module identified→version matched→operational environment matched→approved mode confirmed→patches & dependencies reconciled→claim supported

Any step the public record cannot close reads as additional evidence required, not as a finding.

What the corpus can establish

  • the validated module identity and certificate status
  • the documented operational environments and approved services
  • the declared module boundary, interfaces, and dependencies where stated
  • the public validation-update history recorded on the certificate

What still requires deployment evidence

  • the module version actually installed
  • whether vendor patches changed code inside the validated boundary
  • whether fixes were backported without changing the reported version
  • whether the product operates the module in approved mode
  • whether the consuming service invokes the validated implementation
  • whether the deployed environment is listed or covered by portability rules

How to read the clues

Every signal on the site sits in one of three layers, and they are not interchangeable.

Public fact

Stated directly in the certificate metadata or the Security Policy: module identity, status, operational environments, approved services.

Derived review clue

A deterministic inference from the structured public evidence, or a keyword detected in the text. A prompt to confirm, not a conclusion.

Deployment-dependent conclusion

Needs vendor, product, SBOM, package, or operator evidence. It cannot be reached from the public corpus alone.

Public clues relevant to deployed-state review

Signals extracted from Security Policies and certificate records that may mark an area requiring confirmation. These are review prompts, not findings that a component is exposed, vulnerable, or inside the validated boundary. A single module can raise several; the counts are of modules, not of confirmed issues.
