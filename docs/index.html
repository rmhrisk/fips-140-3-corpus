What you can learn here

The CMVP certificate remains the authoritative validation record. This site restructures that public information so a reviewer can more quickly understand the validated claim and locate the questions that still require vendor or deployment evidence.

What was validated

The module identity, version, environment, approved services, and certificate status documented in the public record.

What has changed publicly

Certificate updates, elapsed time, successor clues, and upstream maintenance activity that may need to be reconciled.

What remains unknown

The installed module version, patch provenance, approved-mode configuration, actual consuming services, and whether changes occurred inside the validated boundary.

Establishing a deployed claim runs a chain; any step the public record cannot close reads as additional evidence required, not as a finding.

certificate found→deployed module identified→version matched→operational environment matched→approved mode confirmed→patches & dependencies reconciled→claim supported

Methodology & limitations

The corpus is a sampled certificate-number sweep (#4700 to #5157, step 3), so it does not represent every FIPS 140-3 validation, and absence of a successor or update entry does not prove none exists. Every signal sits in one of three layers, and they are not interchangeable:

Public fact

Stated directly in the certificate metadata or the Security Policy.

Derived review clue

A deterministic inference from structured public evidence, or a keyword detected in the text. A prompt to confirm, not a conclusion.

Deployment-dependent conclusion

Needs vendor, product, SBOM, package, or operator evidence; it cannot be reached from the public corpus alone.

The result is not a vulnerability verdict. It is a deployed-assurance workflow: identify the validated claim, test its correspondence to the deployed state, and turn each unresolved gap into a specific evidence request.
